Water Conservation

Water is an essential resource that we must take care of. At our university, we face challenges with excessive use of this resource, and we are implementing measures to improve efficiency in its consumption.

  • Installation of water-saving systems in restrooms and cafeterias.
  • Educational campaigns for efficient water use.
  • Monitoring water consumption to detect leaks and correct them quickly.

Additionally, we encourage students and staff to adopt water-saving habits in their daily lives, such as taking shorter showers, fixing leaks promptly, and using water-efficient appliances.

Tips to Save Water in your Home

1. Turn Off the Tap

Don't let the water run while washing your hands or brushing your teeth. You'll save liters of water every day.

2. Use the Shower Smartly

Reduce your shower time and use water-saving showerheads.

3. Fix Leaks

A small drip can waste over 30 liters of water a day. Report and fix any leaks you find.

4. Reuse Water

Water used to wash fruits and vegetables can be reused to water plants.
